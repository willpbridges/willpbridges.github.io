Bird Brigade

A team of elite bird agents takes on a challenging bullet-hell boss rush.

About the Game

Bird Brigade is a 2D bullet-hell boss-rush created for a semester-long class project. The game is viewed from a top-down perspective, with movement assigned to the directional keys and shooting and special abilities assigned to the mouse. There are three player classes (parrot, penguin, and falcon), each with their own respective abilities: dodging, shielding, and rapid-fire. All abilities have a cooldown and must be used strategically. Some boss fights have multiple phases with increased difficulty in each new phase. (We are using this art until we create our own assets.)

The Process

Initially, my team decided we wanted to make a game based on 2D rogue-lites, a genre we were all familiar with. We were very mindful of scope and wrote out a development plan that we knew we could stick to. We started with a very basic shooting and movement prototype, then implemented one boss for playtesting. We realized at playtesting that the game had become a bullet-hell game so we continued development with that in mind. I put in a lot of work at the end of the project to implement our classes and finish the boss fights, and we accomplished our final checkpoint goals. We've done some work on the game since our final presentation, and I really like the scenario and characters we've created, so I'd love to keep working on it.

My Contributions

I was the lead programmer and designer on the project. My team members were very helpful in hashing out game ideas and implementing things like game math and UI, but I implemented most of the core mechanics, including player actions, bosses, and the tutorial level. I decided on the theme of the game and the player classes, and then I implemented their powerups. Properly implementing the dodge and getting the right feel was possibly the most challenging part of development. I also wrote the math and logic that went into the bosses' movement and shooting patterns. In addition, I created our character sprites and wrote a story for the game that will eventually be implemented.
