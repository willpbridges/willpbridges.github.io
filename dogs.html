Dogs Day

A heartwarming adventure game about man's best friend.

About the Game

Dogs Day is a 2D narrative-driven adventure game. You play as a reclusive PhD student who gets brought out of his shell after meeting three lost dogs and helping them find their homes. After meeting each dog, you must explore the map to find special items that will trigger events when brought to certain locations. The game ends when you realize the third dog, a goofy-looking street dog, belongs at home with you.

The Process

Dogs Day was developed in 48 hours for the 2019 Global Game Jam. The theme was "What Home Means To You," and all of my team members associated dogs with home. On Friday night, we made a loose plan of the systems we would develop and what assets we would need. On Saturday, three programmers (including myself) developed the game systems while two artists produced our game's art and our writer wrote the script. On Sunday, we fixed any remaining bugs and created a working build of the game. The game is unfinished, but we have worked individually on it since the jam, and everything is in place for the game to be finished.

My Contributions

This was one of my first Unity projects, and I learned a lot over the course of development. After being active during the brainstorming phase, I developed the first version of our dog behavior script, which determined how each dog would follow you throughout the game world. This script was later tweaked and then individualized for each dog based on their personalities. My main task during the game jam was developing an inventory system, in which items would be added to your inventory and then appear on the inventory screen. Players can move through the inventory with the directional keys, and the selected item is enlarged.
